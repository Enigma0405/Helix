# FDA 21 CFR Part 211 (cGMP for Finished Pharmaceuticals)

## Why Helix Cares
Part 211 is the foundation of Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) in the United States. It dictates how products must be manufactured, tested, and released. Every deviation and CAPA processed by Helix must be evaluated against these requirements to ensure patient safety and legal compliance.

## How AI Uses It
Helix's Knowledge Agent embeds the subparts of Part 211 and uses semantic search to cross-reference deviations against regulatory text. 
When an investigation occurs, Helix automatically flags which subparts are at risk (e.g., a temperature excursion flags Subpart D: Equipment, and Subpart J: Records and Reports).

## How it Affects Investigations
- **Subpart B (Organization and Personnel)**: If an operator causes a deviation, Helix checks training records to ensure compliance with 211.25.
- **Subpart D (Equipment)**: If a machine fails, Helix checks calibration and maintenance logs to ensure compliance with 211.67.
- **Subpart J (Records and Reports)**: Helix uses this to justify why a batch cannot be released if ALCOA+ principles are violated in the batch record (211.188).
- **Subpart I (Laboratory Controls)**: Used to assess Out of Specification (OOS) investigations.
