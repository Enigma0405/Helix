# SOP-DEV-001: Deviation Management and Reporting

## Purpose
To establish the process for the identification, documentation, investigation, and resolution of deviations that occur during manufacturing, testing, and storage of products at Apex Precision Biologics.

## Scope
This SOP applies to all GMP-regulated activities across all facilities.

## Classification
- **Minor**: Does not affect product quality (e.g., typographical error in batch record).
- **Major**: Potential impact to product quality or GMP compliance (e.g., parameter excursion outside validated range).
- **Critical**: Definite impact to product quality, patient safety, or severe regulatory non-compliance (e.g., sterility failure).

## Procedure
1. **Identification**: Operator identifies the deviation and halts the process if necessary.
2. **Initial Reporting**: Operator logs the deviation in the MES or Quality Management System within 24 hours.
3. **Triage & Classification**: QA Reviewer assesses the initial report and classifies the severity.
4. **Investigation**: A cross-functional team (or AI EvidenceOps Agent) gathers evidence, reconstructs the timeline, and determines root cause using tools like 5 Whys or Ishikawa.
5. **CAPA Generation**: Corrective and Preventive Actions are formulated to prevent recurrence.
6. **Closure**: QA approves the final investigation report and tracks CAPA implementation.

## Regulatory Reference
- FDA 21 CFR 211.192 (Production record review).
- ICH Q9 (Quality Risk Management).
